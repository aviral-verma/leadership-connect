Key Strategies

Key Strategies

  • Deposits as a preferred source of funds - Since BFL can not open savings/current account, deposits are the next best source of funds.
  • Managing operational costs - The minimum amount of deposit has consistently decreased from its inception to 25,000 Rs, keeping in mind the operational costs are covered.
  • Tapping low income strata - Although the average deposit amount is more than 1,00,000 Rs, Systematic Deposit plan(SDP) is introduced to tap the lower income strata whose minimum monthly installment is 5,000 Rs.
  • Higher interest rate on deposits - Since NBFCs are not covered like banks, BFL keeps the interest rates higher than other banks to lure the customers.
  • New products - Based on the feedback of the customers, single maturity plan has been introduced.

FACTORS AFFECTING INTEREST RATES

FACTORS AFFECTING INTEREST RATES

  • Market activity affects the interest rate.

  • Varies directly with the monetary policy adopted by the RBI.

  • Varies directly with the rate at which RBI lends money to commercial banks, i.e. repo rate.

  • A cut in the repo rate implies a decline in the Fixed Deposits interest rates and vice versa.

Product - Loan Against Fixed Deposits

Product - Loan Against Fixed Deposits

  • BFL provides loan against Fixed Deposits of upto 75% of the principal deposit
  • The interest rate for such loans is 2% more than that offered on deposit
  • BFL has capped the loan at 75% of deposit as per the Circular from RBI
  • Banks provide loan against Fixed deposits of upto 90% of the principal deposit because banks have liquidity from Current and Savings accounts also apart from the term deposits.
  • The 75% cap ensures that there is no ALM (Asset Liability Mismatch).

Business Model of Insurance
